Software

Introduction

As scientists we constantly need to modify or develop software in order to better analyze our data. As our team develops new tools or tweaks existing programs to do useful things, we will be updating this page to contribute to the growing body of bioinformatic software.

Assembly of sequence data

There is a wealth of data existing on public databases as well as within our novel genome/transcriptome data. Here we will archive bioinformatic approaches to dataset assembly, spanning topics that include assembly of large-scale virus datasets and the identification and harvesting of immune receptor orthologs between evolutionarily divergent taxa

Immunogenetic Data

Assessing residues, selection, synteny, etc are routine in our immunogenetic work. Here we archive code to accomplish these tasks

Phylogenetic comparative methods

There are numerous approaches to analyzing trait data on phylogenies. Here we will archive walkthroughs of scripts to conduct various comparative phylogenetic analyses
